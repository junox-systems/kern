# Kern Manifesto v0.2

## Definition

**Kern is a personal kernel for attention.**

Its purpose is not to organize life.

Its purpose is to reduce the cognitive cost of deciding what deserves attention now.

It does not optimize productivity.

It optimizes clarity.

---

# First Principle

Attention is finite.

Reality is dynamic.

Decision is expensive.

Therefore:

**Decide as little as possible.**

---

# Axiom I — Reality

Reality is the only truth.

Time passes.

Energy changes.

Interruptions happen.

Commitments appear.

Commitments finish.

The kernel never argues with reality.

It only observes.

---

# Axiom II — State

Every commitment exists in exactly one state.

State describes what is true.

Not what is hoped.

Not what is planned.

Reality changes state.

The kernel reflects it.

---

# Axiom III — Structure

Commitments exist within a dependency DAG.

Nothing exists in isolation.

Every commitment either:

* stands alone,
* depends on another,
* or contains smaller commitments.

The graph defines possibility.

Not priority.

---

# Axiom IV — Policy

Policies are values made explicit.

Policies never invent reality.

Policies never invent importance.

Policies only eliminate what should not happen.

The kernel never asks:

> "What is best?"

It asks:

> "Given reality, what remains?"

---

# Axiom V — Decision

A plan is disposable.

A decision is immediate.

The kernel never plans a life.

It continuously produces the smallest valid next decision.

Nothing more.

---

# The Loop

Observe.

Model.

Eliminate.

Act.

Repeat.

---

# Reduction

Every cycle removes uncertainty.

Reality creates possibility.

Structure removes impossibility.

Policy removes inappropriateness.

One decision remains.

---

# Simplicity

Complexity belongs inside the kernel.

Simplicity belongs outside it.

The user should never experience the complexity required to produce a decision.

---

# Knowledge

Knowledge exists only to improve future decisions.

Knowledge is not collected.

Knowledge is compressed.

Anything that does not reduce future decisions is discarded.

---

# Planning

Planning is compilation against current reality.

Every interruption invalidates assumptions.

Therefore every plan is ephemeral.

Only knowledge persists.

---

# Progress

Progress is not measured by completed commitments.

Progress is measured by reduced ambiguity.

Every completed decision makes the next decision easier.

---

# Failure

Failure is information.

It is neither moral nor personal.

It is simply another observation about reality.

The kernel learns reality.

It never judges the operator.

---

# Constraint

Freedom emerges from constraint.

The kernel does not maximize options.

It removes options until action becomes obvious.

---

# Attention

Time is not the scarce resource.

Attention is.

Time cannot be scheduled.

Only attention can.

---

# The Kernel

The kernel owns only three things.

Reality.

Structure.

Policy.

Everything else is derived.

---

# Complexity Budget

Every permanent concept is debt.

A concept exists only if it permanently reduces future decisions.

Otherwise it is removed.

The kernel grows by subtraction.

---

# The Law of Elimination

The kernel never chooses.

It eliminates.

First what cannot happen.

Then what should not happen.

Until only one reasonable action remains.

---

# The Law of Locality

The kernel concerns itself only with the present decision.

The future emerges from repeated present decisions.

---

# The Law of Sovereignty

The operator owns values.

The kernel owns execution.

The kernel never decides what matters.

It only executes the operator's values consistently under changing reality.

---

# The Law of Transparency

Every decision must be explainable.

Nothing is hidden.

Nothing is magical.

Nothing requires faith.

---

# The Law of Adaptation

Reality changes continuously.

The kernel responds continuously.

Adaptation comes from observation.

Not prediction.

---

# The Law of Reduction

The purpose of Kern is not to make life more organized.

It is to make life require fewer decisions.

---

# Final Definition

Kern is a deterministic attention kernel built upon state, structure, and explicit policy.

It continuously observes reality, models executable possibility through an acyclic dependency graph, removes impossible and inappropriate actions through transparent policies, and presents the smallest valid next decision.

Its measure of success is not productivity.

It is the progressive elimination of unnecessary decisions.
